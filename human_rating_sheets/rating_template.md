# Human Rating Sheets

## Instructions for Raters

You will be shown outputs from 3 systems (labeled A, B, C).
**You do NOT know which system produced which output.**
Rate each output on a scale of 1-5.

---

## T3: Hindi Image Captioning

For each image, you will see 3 Hindi captions. Rate each on:

**Accuracy (1-5):** Does the caption correctly describe what's in the image?
- 1 = Completely wrong / irrelevant
- 2 = Mentions the image but major errors
- 3 = Roughly correct but missing key details
- 4 = Accurate with minor omissions
- 5 = Perfectly accurate description

**Naturalness (1-5):** Does the Hindi read naturally?
- 1 = Broken / unreadable Hindi
- 2 = Understandable but clearly machine-generated
- 3 = Acceptable Hindi, some awkward phrasing
- 4 = Natural Hindi, minor issues
- 5 = Reads like a fluent Hindi speaker wrote it

## T4: Blog Headline Translation (English → Hindi)

For each English headline, you will see 2-3 Hindi translations. Rate each on:

**Essence Captured (1-5):** Does the Hindi version capture the meaning AND the tone/impact of the English headline?
- 1 = Meaning lost entirely
- 2 = Literal translation that misses the point
- 3 = Meaning preserved but feels flat
- 4 = Good translation, captures most of the impact
- 5 = Excellent — a Hindi reader would find this as compelling as the English original

**Audience Appeal (1-5):** Would a Hindi-speaking policy reader want to click on this headline?
- 1 = Confusing or off-putting
- 2 = Understandable but bland
- 3 = Decent headline
- 4 = Engaging headline
- 5 = Compelling — would definitely read the article
